What is this?

The open group is for anyone — advanced high-school students, undergraduates, early researchers, or curious people with enough background to read primary literature — who wants to work through a set of research papers together.

Each cohort reads four papers over four weeks: one 1.5-hour session per week. The topic varies by cohort and is chosen to be self-contained — a cluster of papers that hang together and reward close reading.

Four papers is a survey of sorts — but a very partial one, chosen to hang together. The goal is to read each carefully enough to know what it actually claims, what it doesn't, and what you would need to believe to find it convincing.

Current track
NeuroAI — Minds, Machines & Visual Cortex

Four papers at the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning: what deep networks reveal about the brain, what the brain reveals about learning algorithms, and why the question of "understanding" a complex system is harder than it looks. Led by an active researcher in the field.

What you'll gain
  • Practice reading primary literature closely — what a paper claims, what it doesn't, and what it assumes
  • A personal next-steps map: adjacent papers, methods worth understanding, open questions to sit with
  • Alumni Discord access (announcements + ongoing discussion)
