What is this?

Each cohort — just 2–3 students — works through a serious text together over four weeks: one 1.5-hour session per week, with guided reading in between.

The goal is not to finish a syllabus. It is to get students started on how to read hard material on their own, ask sharp questions, and use AI as a thinking partner — not a shortcut.

Current tracks
Physics
The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. 1

Start from atoms in motion. Work toward gravity, probability, and the edges of what classical physics can explain. No calculus required — we'll work from selected chapters and keep the math lightweight, but the reasoning won't.

Mathematics
Proofs from the Book — Aigner & Ziegler

Six proofs that primes are infinite. The irrationality of √2, e, and π. The Pigeonhole Principle. Euler's formula for polyhedra. Each chapter is a small miracle — a surprising result proved in a way that seems, in retrospect, inevitable.

AI & Cognitive Neuroscience
Vehicles — Valentino Braitenberg

Fourteen imaginary machines of increasing complexity. Vehicle 1 has two wires. Vehicle 14 has something that looks uncomfortably like a personality. Nothing magical happens in between — and that's the point. When does mechanism become mind? This track is led by an active researcher in the field.

What you'll gain
  • Practice in close reading: definition-making, proof/derivation hygiene, and sanity-checking your own reasoning
  • A personal next-steps map: what to read next, how to choose resources, and how to self-test
  • Alumni Discord access (peer group + announcements)

For parents / guardians

What happens after you send this

I read every form. If it seems like a good fit, I'll reach out within a week to set up a short call — 15 minutes, mostly so you can ask me questions and I can get a sense of what the student is curious about in conversation.

If I think it's not the right fit right now, I'll say so honestly.

Cohorts form when I have 2–3 students with overlapping interests. The wait is usually a few weeks. Fees are discussed on the call — waivers or reductions can be discussed.

Safeguarding & terms
  • For students under 18: a parent/guardian must consent; they're welcome to observe sessions.
  • During the track, all communication stays in server channels — no private DMs between mentor and students. Participants may connect independently after the track ends.
  • Mentor replies happen in the course channel, not 24/7 — but questions don't go unanswered.
