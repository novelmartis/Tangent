Back to Tracks See Foundational Track
Who it is for
  • Advanced high school students (usually Grades 11–12 working beyond typical expectations) and undergraduate students.
Current topic

NeuroAI — Minds, Machines & Visual Cortex

Four papers at the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning. We ask what networks reveal about the brain, what the brain reveals about learning, and what remains hard to explain.

Future topics open based on interest overlap and mentor availability.

What we read
  • One paper per week.
  • Sometimes a technical essay or review alongside it.
  • Optional extra reading for students who want more depth.
What students practise
  • What is the question?
  • What is the main claim?
  • How does the argument or evidence work?
  • What remains unclear?
4-week format
  • Week 0: short intro call before the reading cycle begins.
  • Week 1: mentor models how to read and present a paper.
  • Weeks 2–4: students present and lead part of discussion.
  • In larger groups, students may work in pairs or trios on a paper.
  • A simple 1-slide format is enough.

Good questions matter. The point is not polished performance.
