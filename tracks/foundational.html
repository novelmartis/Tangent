Foundational Track

For younger students who want depth, not just speed.

Small groups, one core reading each week, and discussion that teaches students how to think through hard material.

Grades 7–9 (or equivalent) 2–3 students 4 weeks
Back to Tracks See Advanced Track
Who it is for
  • Grades 7–9 students, or equivalent level.
  • Serious younger students are welcome.
  • Curiosity matters more than polish.
  • Good for students who want depth, not just speed.
Current topics

Physics

The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. 1

Start from atoms in motion and work toward gravity, probability, and the limits of classical ideas.

Mathematics

Proofs from the Book — Aigner & Ziegler

Beautiful proofs that train taste, clarity, and mathematical reasoning.

AI & Cognitive Neuroscience

Vehicles — Valentino Braitenberg

From simple mechanisms to behavior that starts to look like mind.

If the right group forms, additional topics can be opened.

These are not easy texts; the point is not speed, but learning how to stay with hard ideas.

4-week format
  • One chapter or core reading each week.
  • Four sessions total.
  • Session 1 is usually mentor-led.
  • Later sessions become more student-led.
What students practise
  • What is this chapter trying to explain?
  • What are the key ideas or principles?
  • How do the examples or steps help?
  • What still feels confusing or incomplete?
