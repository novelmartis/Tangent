Foundational Track

For younger students who want depth, not just speed.

Small groups, one core reading each week, and discussion that builds habits for hard material.

Middle school / early secondary Roughly Grades 7–10 3–4 students 4 weeks
Back to Tracks See Advanced Track
Who it is for
  • Middle school / early secondary students (roughly Grades 7–10).
  • Best suited to students ready for sustained reading, questioning, and discussion.
  • Curiosity matters more than polish.
Current topics

Physics

The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. 1

Start from simple assumptions and build toward deeper ideas in motion, gravity, and uncertainty.

Mathematics

Proofs from the Book — Aigner & Ziegler

Elegant proofs that build mathematical taste and the move from intuition to clear argument.

Minds, Brains, and Machines

Vehicles — Valentino Braitenberg

From simple mechanisms to complex behavior, with focus on explanation and emergence.

If the right group forms, additional topics can be opened.

These are not easy texts; the point is not speed, but learning how to stay with hard ideas.

4-week format
  • Week 0: short intro/fit call before reading begins.
  • Weeks 1–4: one chapter or core reading each week.
  • Session 1 is usually mentor-led.
  • Later sessions become more student-led.
What students practise
  • What is this chapter trying to explain?
  • What are the key ideas or principles?
  • How do the examples or steps help?
  • What still feels confusing or incomplete?
