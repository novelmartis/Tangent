Foundational Track

A small reading group for students who want depth.

The Foundational Track is designed for students in Grades 7–12 who are ready to read more carefully, think more clearly, and discuss ideas rather than just collect answers.

Grades 7–12 3–4 students 4 weeks Online
Apply Meet the mentors
Who it suits

Good fit

Students who are curious, can tolerate difficulty, and want to understand what they are reading rather than move through it quickly.

Less about polish

Students do not need to sound advanced or already know how to discuss books well. The group is meant to help them grow into that.

Subjects

Many starting points.

The exact reading depends on the group, but these are the three current directions and some of the books or chapters they may draw from.

Physics

The Feynman Lectures on Physics

Selected chapters and related excerpts for students interested in motion, force, explanation, and what physics is trying to say.

Mathematics

Proofs from THE BOOK — Aigner & Ziegler

For students interested in proof, elegance, and learning how mathematical arguments become convincing.

Intelligent Behavior

Vehicles — Valentino Braitenberg

For students interested in intelligence, behavior, and how simple mechanisms can generate surprising complexity.

If the right cluster of interest forms, Tangent can shape a group around a nearby topic.

What students do

Reading and preparation

  • One core reading each week.
  • Students prepare around a few guiding questions.
  • The aim is to notice what matters and what is still unclear.

Discussion and growth

  • Sessions are live, online, and small-group.
  • Earlier meetings are more guided; later ones ask more of the students.
  • The atmosphere should feel thoughtful and non-performative.
