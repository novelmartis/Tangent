Mentors

Meet the people guiding the groups.

Tangent mentors are not there to rush students or over-explain everything. They are there to help students stay with difficult ideas, ask better questions, and grow into stronger thinkers.

What mentors do

Choose the material well

Mentors select readings that are demanding enough to be worth discussing, but workable for the students in the group.

Guide the discussion

They help students sort out what is central, what is confusing, and what deserves further thought.

Take students seriously

The point is not to flatten the material. It is to meet students at a high level while still supporting them well.
