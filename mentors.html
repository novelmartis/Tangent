Dr. Sushrut Thorat
Founding mentor · ML & Cognitive Neuroscience

Researcher in machine learning and cognitive neuroscience. His work studies how the brain represents the visual world, and how artificial neural networks can be made to better reflect biological vision.

Has supervised 20+ students across undergraduate, masters, and PhD levels, and run reading groups for years — at university and informally with students in India. Several of those informal mentees went on to competitive undergraduate and master's programmes.

Currently based in India; moving to Sydney (MARCS Institute, Western Sydney University) in 2026. Sessions are online.

Run a track

Tangent is looking for researchers and domain experts who want to run reading groups of their own. If you have a subject you think deeply about and a small set of texts worth reading carefully — that's enough to start.

We handle the structure, the students, and the logistics. You design the reading list and lead the sessions.

Payout and terms are discussed on a call — they depend on the track, the group size, and what you're comfortable with.
