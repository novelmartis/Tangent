Tracks

Choose the track that matches where you are right now.

Both tracks run for four weeks in small groups with a mentor. The main difference is what you read and how technical the discussion gets.

Track overview

Foundational and Advanced

Foundational

Middle school / early secondary Roughly Grades 7–10 3–4 students 4 weeks

For younger students ready for depth beyond standard classroom pace. Reading centers on textbook chapters and foundational material.

  • Good for students who want depth without performance pressure.
  • Themes often include Physics, Mathematics, and Minds, Brains, and Machines.
  • Current offerings: Feynman Lectures, Proofs from the Book, and Minds, Brains, and Machines (Vehicles).
Go to Foundational Track →

Advanced

Advanced high school / undergraduate 4–5 students 4 weeks

For advanced high school and undergraduate students ready for paper-based discussion. Reading centers on one research paper each week, sometimes with a technical essay or review.

  • Best for students who want to examine arguments and evidence directly.
  • Student-led mini-presentations increase as the group progresses.
  • Current offering: NeuroAI (minds, machines, and visual cortex).
Go to Advanced Track →
