How It Works

From application to weekly discussion.

Apply, talk with us, get placed in the right group, read each week, and meet for careful discussion.

The process

From inquiry to group.

1

Families apply through the Apply page.

2

We schedule a short call to understand the student, answer questions, and see whether the group is a good fit.

3

Students are grouped by readiness, interests, and timing.

4

Before the four-week group begins, we hold an introductory session: we introduce the book and the intention of the group, speak with the student and parent, and set up the four content sessions.

5

The group then runs over four weeks, with core reading and one live session each week.

What a week looks like

Before the session

Students do the week's reading and prepare around a few simple questions: what is being explained, what matters most, and what remains unclear.

During the session

The group works through the reading together. The emphasis is on explanation, questions, difficulty, and clarity rather than performance.

Across the month

Early sessions are usually more mentor-led. Over time, students are expected to speak more clearly, ask better questions, and carry more of the conversation.

What we look for

Curiosity and steadiness

We are not looking for polished students. We are looking for students who care about understanding and can stay with something even when it gets difficult.

Good group fit

Not every interested student belongs in the same group. We try to place students where the material, pace, and conversation will genuinely work for them.

Format
  • Small groups, usually 3–4 students.
  • An introductory setup session before the four-week group begins.
  • Four weeks.
  • Online by default.
  • English by default unless a shared language and mentor fit make another language possible.
  • Pricing and subsidy details are discussed after fit and placement are clear.
