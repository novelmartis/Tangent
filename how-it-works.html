How it Works

Simple structure, small groups, serious reading.

Students are not expected to already know how to read hard material well. The process teaches this gradually.

Why this structure

Small groups, hard texts, gradual autonomy.

Many students can do more serious thinking than standard formats usually allow. Tangent keeps the structure small and deliberate.

  • Start with serious material.
  • Read before meeting.
  • Move from guided discussion toward student-led thinking.
Before the group starts
1

Open the Foundational or Advanced page and fill the form there.

2

We schedule a short call to assess fit and readiness.

3

Students are grouped by level, interests, and timing.

4

Selected students join a short intro call before week 1.

If immediate placement is not possible, students may be waitlisted and still invited to the Discord community.

The four-week structure

One core text each week.

  • Week 0: short intro/fit call before the reading cycle begins.
  • Weeks 1–4: one core text each week, with preparation before each session.
  • Sessions are live, online, and small-group.
What preparation looks like

Book chapters (Foundational)

  • What is this chapter trying to explain?
  • What are the central ideas?
  • Which examples or steps matter most?
  • What still feels confusing?

Research papers (Advanced)

  • What is the question?
  • What is the main claim?
  • How do they support that claim?
  • What remains unclear?
What happens in a session
1

Brief opening and context.

2

Short presentation from mentor or students.

3

Group discussion of what is clear, what is weak, and what is confusing.

4

Mentor helps sort out the hard parts and open questions.

  • Session 1 is usually more mentor-led; later sessions become more student-led.
  • One slide is enough.
  • The point is not polished performance, but learning how to think, explain, question, and stay with hard material.
  • Tangents are welcome, but they stay tied to the reading.
  • Where relevant, groups may also discuss how to use modern AI tools well: as tools to direct, inspect, and learn from, not substitutes for understanding or judgment.
Online format
  • All groups are online by default.
  • Timing depends on mentor availability and group formation.
  • Final scheduling is worked out after interest is registered.
  • Sessions run in English by default unless a mentor can comfortably run a cohort in another language shared by the group.
A note on the philosophy

Tangent is built on a simple belief: many students can do more serious thinking than they are usually invited into. This structure makes that possible without turning it into pressure or performance.

After the four weeks
  • Students can remain in the Discord space.
  • Discussion, paper sharing, and group updates continue there.
